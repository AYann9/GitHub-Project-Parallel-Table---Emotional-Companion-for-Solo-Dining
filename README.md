## 1. Overview
**Parallel Table** is a screen‑free, hardware‑software integrated emotional companion system built for young solo dwellers. It covers the full solo‑dining journey: **meal prep → dining → cleanup**, solving three core pain points:
- Prep anxiety
- Dining loneliness
- Post‑meal fatigue

It uses invisible interaction and asynchronous light social to deliver calm, private, pressure‑free healing experiences.

## 2. Background & Market Opportunity
### 2.1 Industry Trend (iiMedia 2024)
- 53.19% choose convenient / pre‑cooked food
- 39.77% choose food delivery
- 19.13% dine out alone

Most products only solve **convenience**, not **emotion & experience**.

### 2.2 User Signals
- Users make manual cooking schedules to handle multitask anxiety
- High consumption of eating broadcasts for low‑pressure companionship
- Young people prefer private, undisturbed healing space

### 2.3 Opportunity
Upgrade from **efficiency tools** to **emotional value products** and fill emotional gaps across the entire solo dining process.

## 3. User Research & Insights
### 3.1 Methods
- Shadowing observation
- In‑depth interviews
- Desk research (Bilibili, Xiaohongshu, reports)
- Cross‑industry experience economy analysis

### 3.2 Target Users
- Age: 22–30
- Status: young professionals, renting, occasional cooking
- Traits: quality‑oriented, social anxiety / low social desire, need emotional comfort

### 3.3 Core Pain Points
| Stage | Functional Pain | Emotional Pain |
|-------|-----------------|----------------|
| Prep | Multitask, time chaos, info overload | Anxiety, irritation, fatigue |
| Dining | Cold space, no ritual, screen distraction | Loneliness, lack of companionship |
| Cleanup | Boring chores, no reward | Low mood, no achievement |

### 3.4 Key Insights
1. Prep needs **flow & light feedback**, not heavy gamification.
2. Dining table **rejects screens**; invisible interaction builds psychological safety.
3. Solo users want **one‑way safe companionship** without social pressure.

### 3.5 POV
Tired young solo dwellers urgently need screen‑free invisible sensory care and pressure‑free asynchronous companionship during solo dining, to achieve controllable healing through minimal physical rituals.

## 5. Product Solution
### 5.1 Positioning
A decoupled hardware‑software emotional compensation system for solo dining.

**Core components**:
- Flexible electronic table mat
- NFC tableware
- Mood‑based blind‑box algorithm

### 5.2 Core Modules
| Module | Interaction | Emotional Value |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| Gamified Prep Guide | Step breakdown, light progress bar, soft audio | Reduce anxiety, create flow |
| Flexible Electronic Mat | Pressure sensing, breathing light | Invisible interaction, aesthetic shelter |
| NFC Smart Tableware | Trigger mood blind‑box (white noise + narrative) | Zero‑effort ritual, understood companionship |
| Mood Recipe Blind Box | Scan ingredients → emotional script | Safe one‑way companionship |
| Post‑Meal Narrative | Story audio during dishwashing | Chores → immersive theater, positive reward |

### 5.3 Experience Economy 4 Quadrants
- **Entertainment**: Passive + absorb → ambient sound by mood/weather
- **Educational**: Active + absorb → prep step breakdown, light guidance
- **Esthetic**: Passive + immerse → mat lighting, screen‑free calm
- **Escapist**: Active + immerse → mood blind box, dishwashing story

## 10. Interaction Flow (Core)
### NFC Tableware → Mood Blind Box
1. Place tableware on mat
2. Pressure sensor detects weight
3. NFC reads UID
4. System matches today mood tag
5. Pull mood audio from cloud
6. Mat breathing light syncs rhythm
7. Audio plays
8. Remove tableware → light fades
